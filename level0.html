Level 0: Resources

Collated by the creators of Healthut

You are not alone. There are others who understand what you're going through and want to help. If there is someone you trust, talk to them about what you're struggling with. If you don't have anyone to talk to, use the resources below to find someone. You can do it.

What’s Up Walk-in
1-866-585-6486
Free Virtual and In-Person Mental Health Counseling.

Naseeha Organization
1-866-627-3342
A helpline that is specifically designed to address the unique mental health challenges faced by Muslims. Offers confidential and non-judgmental support to those in need. Those of different faiths or no faith are welcome to use this helpline as well.

Black Youth Helpline
416-285-9944
Helpline designed specifically for Black youth, but serves all young people.

Children’s Mental Health Organization
Network of child and youth mental health centers that can direct you to counseling and support. Does not directly provide mental health services.

The Trevor Project
They offer many kinds of support such as a helpline you can call, text, or message, as well as spaces to meet LGBTQ+ friends (virtually), learn about LGBTQ+ topics, and more.
